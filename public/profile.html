2. OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH SERVICES

2.1 Occupational Medicine

  • The OMP(Occupational Medical Practitioner) shall develop and maintain a comprehensive HRA (Health Risk Assessment) program in conjunction with the Occupational Hygienist, taking in account risk assessments, legislative requirements and specific health needs of the company

  • Medical Screening/Biological Monitoring

  • Clinical Medical services for Occupational Injuries/Disease Management

2.2 Occupational Hygiene

  • The anticipation, recognition, evaluation and control of conditions at a mine/industry that may cause illness or adverse effects to persons in conjunction with Occupational Hygienist

2.3 Medical Surveillance

  • A planned programme of periodic examination, which may include clinical examinations, biological monitoring or medical testing of employees by an Occupational Health Practitioner or Occupational Medical Practitioner.

  • Biological Monitoring is a planned programme of periodic collection of body fluid, tissues, excreta or exhaled air in order to detect and quantify the exposure to, or absorption of any substance or organism

2.4 Medical Examinations

  • Medical Examinations, which form part of Medical Surveillance, will be conducted at the on-site clinic or at Kalaharimed(PTY)Ltd, 58 Jan Fiskaal Street, Kathu

2.4.1 Pre-Employment Medical

  • A pre-employment examination will be performed to make a decision on medical suitability for employment

2.4.2 Periodic/Annual Medical Examination

  • Perform the periodic/annual medical examination for all employees as required by the risk-based medical surveillance protocol and assess according to approved minimum medical dtandard of fitness

  • Record all information obtained from the exeminations in the employee`s medical file as confidential

  • Issue a Cerficate of Fitness to perform work in relation to the minimum medical criteria for that specific job

2.4.3 Pre-Placement/Transfer Medical Examinations

  • Assessment of medical suitability in relation to minimum medical standards for any change in job

2.4.4 Exit Medical

  • An Exit medical examination will be performed on all employees whose service is terminated and scheduling will be prioritized

  • Exit medical examinations will be conducted at the on-site clinic or at Kalaharimed centre in Kathu, before the employee exit from the site

  • No Exit medical examination will be performed without a completed and signed-off `Record of Hazardous Work` form

  • Outstanding medical insurance issues identified, or possible occupational disease cases, will be referred to clients for finalization

  • The following will be provided to the terminating employee; -An Exit Medical Certificate -A Record of Hazardous work

2.4.5 Periodic Medical

  • Perform a periodic medical examination for all employees as required by the risk based medical surveillance protocol and assess according to approved minimum medical standard of fitness

  • Record al information obtained from the examinations in the employee`s medical file as confidential

  • Issue a Certificate of Fitness to perform work in relation to the minimum medical standards for any change in job

2.4.6 Special Occupational Health Requirements

  • Pregnancy Programme for Women in high Risk areas as classified. This will include risk assessments and monthly monitoring to ensure compliance to Code of Practice as set out in legislation

  • Screening/Testing for COVID-19 before starting with observations/tests/examinations

  • Fatique monitoring programme

  • Review and Implementation of Drug test Policy

  • TB screening will be part of the medical surveillance program

  • Health awareness will be done as per needs of client e.g. HIV/AIDS, TB and STI`s

  • All employees, who received an unfit certificate, should be personally informed by the OHP/OMP

  • Monthly hygiene inspections to be conducted and reported at Health and Safety (H/S) meetings, if required

  • Monthly H/S meetings to be attended by Kalaharimed personnel as co opted member

2.4.7 Work Related Illness and Injury Management

  • The OMP will assess and review work related injuries

  • Kalaharimed personnel will keep record of all injuries and Occupational diseases, medical investigations, treatment and referral in confidential medical files

  • All work related injuries and Occupational diseases will be reported to the applicable departments of the client

2.4.8 Compliance and Liaison for Occupational Health

  • Weekly, Monthly and Annual Reports will be provided, including the following;

  • Amount of medicals completed, fit/unfit for work

  • Medicals, pre employment, periodical and exit

  • Amount of employees consulted for Primary Health care, hypertension, diabetis, asthma etc

  • Health care education provided

  • Walk through/Inspections done on site

  • Monthly client repot, including all the above

  • Meetings attended, included; - Client meeting, monthly/quarterly, as per client`s requirement - Health and Safety meetings as co opted member

3. PRIMARY HEALTH CARE SERVICES

3.1 Medical Care

  • Minor ailments, where medication is required, shall be managed in the clinic, by the allocted PHC or Wellness Sr. The objective is to ensure that the employee with minor ailments, who requires medication, are treated and can rerurn to their work place

  • when the condition requires further treatment, the patient must be referred to either OMP, own GP, municipal clinic, hospital or Family Planning clinic

  • ensuring ongoing follow-up of chronic disease patients as well as non-compliance

  • referral of complex cases for specialist care will be done when required

  • ensuring professional and confidential medical record keeping at all times

  • ensuring pharmacy adheres to all relevant legislation when MOU is finalized

  • ensuring proper record keeping fot issuing medication as well as medicine registers

  • ensuring Scheduled medicine registers to be counter signed by OMP

3.2 Medical Referrals and Specialist Visits

  • Kalaharimed will facilitate specialist referrals based on medical need, according to client`s requirements. Costs incurred will be for the patient’s own account

  • Kalaharimed will report on monthly referral profile in terms of outcomes

5. EMERGENCY SERVICES

5.1 Emergency Personnel and Ambulance

  • Kalaharimed will provide a professional, effective emergency care service at the clinic

  • An ambulance, stationed at the client will be available

  • Provision of a medical Dr on a 24 hour standby service, seven days a week, will be provided, when required

5.2 Emergency Transportation

  • Kalaharimed Emergency services will attend to all injuries, as well as sick employees on site

  • Emergency evacuation will be authorized and managed by the senior health professional according to medical and logistic circumstances

  • Emergency transportation will be dictated by medical needs and available options in consultation with SHEQ Manager

5.3 Clinic Ambulance

  • Kalaharimed shall be responsible for ensuring that the ambulance, stationed at the clinic, medical equipment, emergency medication, surgical consumables and oxygen are not expired, available and accessible at all times and staff are proficient in the use thereof as well as treatment of emergencies
